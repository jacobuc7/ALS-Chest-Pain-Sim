ALS Chest Pain Simulator

Choose how you want to run the scenario. Both modes use the same patient-care flow; the difference is whether the case and grading are fully random or aligned to continuing-education-style review.

Best experience: use a computer or tablet.

Practice mode

A randomly generated chest-pain case each run—history, vitals, rhythm, and ECG pattern vary like real shifts. Use it to rehearse clinical prioritization, treatments, transport decisions, and debrief feedback without lesson or quiz requirements.

CE mode

Continuing-education track: acknowledge the disclaimer, choose the ECG pattern you want to study (inferior, anterior, lateral, or NSTEMI), then complete the scenario. If you earn a Good score or better, you unlock Lesson & review and the knowledge check for a demo completion screen. Follow your agency’s rules for actual CE credit—this module is a structured teaching path, not a substitute for formal credentialing.

Educational simulation only—not a substitute for protocols, medical direction, or your department’s authorization to practice.

CE mode — acknowledgment

You are entering the continuing-education track. Content is for learning and self-assessment. It does not replace your service’s policies, offline CE requirements, or proof of credit unless your organization explicitly approves this tool.

You will select an ECG scenario type next; the simulation will use that pattern while other patient factors stay randomly generated—mirroring focused drill while preserving realistic variability.
